Introduction¶

In this project, we'll be analyzing the Kaggle No-Show Appointment dataset. The dataset contains 110.527 medical appointments and its 14 associated variables (characteristics) by which we are going to discuss the issue of making a doctor appointment & recieving all the instructions then don't show up.

This report is going to answer the following questions:¶

  • Does the patiant age affect the decision of attending the doctor appointment?
  • Which Neighbourhood's people are more consistant to be on time in their doctor sessions?
  • In which gender the ratio of being on time is heigher?
  • Does the patient's disease has a role in going to doctor? or not?
  • Does the Brazilian social welfare program make people more strict to be on time with their doctor?
  • What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?
  • In which period of the year people decided to make a doctor appointment the most?
  • What is the disease that targeting youth people?

Inferences and Conclusions.¶

We've drawn many inferences from the survey. Here's a summary of a few of them:

  • Based on the provided data, we found that the people's age that around 50 to 60 years old are more consistant to show up in their doctor appointment whereas the large majority of those who didn't come were between the age from 25 to 35 years old.
